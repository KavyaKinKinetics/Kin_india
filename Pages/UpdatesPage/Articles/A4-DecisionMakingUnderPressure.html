Decision-Making Under Pressure - Lessons from Space Missions & Extreme Environments

How do humans make decisions when isolated, stressed, and fatigued? Astronauts, polar researchers, and deep-sea explorers face extreme environments where every decision impacts mission success and safety.

A new study identifies key factors affecting decision-making in ICE (Isolated, Confined, and Extreme) environments:
  • Team dynamics & support structures influence decision confidence.
  • Emotional resilience plays a critical role in high-pressure situations.
  • Technological tools shape cognitive workload and problem-solving efficiency.

Why this matters for space & Earth:

  • AI-driven decision-support systems could enhance astronaut autonomy.
  • Psychological training could help astronauts manage stress and cognitive overload.
  • Lessons from space missions could improve leadership, crisis management, and high-risk professions on Earth.
