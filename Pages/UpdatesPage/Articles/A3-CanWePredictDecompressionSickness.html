Can We Predict Decompression Sickness for Future Lunar and Martian Astronauts?

As we take steps toward long-duration space missions, astronaut health and safety become critical. One major challenge? Decompression Sickness (DCS).

On Earth, we associate DCS with deep-sea diving, but in space, frequent extravehicular activities (EVAs) expose astronauts to sudden pressure changes. Unlike on the ISS, where microgravity dominates, Lunar and Martian EVAs happen in partial gravity, meaning current prediction models may not be fully reliable.

Why does this matter?

  • Improves astronaut safety for Moon & Mars missions
  • Helps in designing better EVA protocols
  • Advances human spaceflight medicine
